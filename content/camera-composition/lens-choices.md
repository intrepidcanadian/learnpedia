Focal length is personality. A 14mm wide-angle and a 200mm telephoto shooting the same subject tell radically different stories.

## Focal Length Guide

### Ultra Wide (14–24mm)

Exaggerated perspective, distortion at edges. Makes spaces feel vast. Use for: epic landscapes, dramatic architecture, disorienting close-ups. **Note:** Extreme wide-angle at close range can distort facial features — avoid for portraiture unless distortion is intentional. Prompt: `"shot on 14mm ultra-wide lens"`

### Wide (24–35mm)

Natural perspective with slight expansion. The documentary lens. Use for: establishing shots, walking interviews, environmental portraits. Prompt: `"shot on 28mm lens"`

### Normal (40–50mm)

Closest to human vision. Neutral, honest. The "invisible" lens — 50mm is the classic "normal" focal length. Use for: dialogue, natural scenes. Prompt: `"shot on 50mm lens"`

### Short Telephoto (85–135mm)

Flattering compression. The portrait lens. Beautiful [depth of field](/wiki/depth-of-field) separation. Use for: character close-ups, interviews. Prompt: `"shot on 85mm lens, shallow depth of field"`

### Telephoto (135–200mm+)

Heavy compression, stacked planes. Isolates subjects from background. Use for: surveillance feel, nature, sports, intimacy from distance. Prompt: `"shot on 200mm telephoto lens"`

### Anamorphic

Not a focal length but a lens type. Produces horizontal lens flares, oval bokeh, and a characteristic "squeezed" image that is typically paired with a widescreen [aspect ratio](/wiki/aspect-ratios-resolution) (2.39:1). The lens character (flares, bokeh shape) is distinct from the aspect ratio choice — you get anamorphic character at any ratio, but the classic cinema look combines both. Prompt: `"anamorphic lens, oval bokeh, horizontal lens flares"`

### Fisheye (8–16mm)

Extreme barrel distortion, 180° field of view. Surreal, immersive, or disorienting. Use for: skateboarding POV, surveillance, psychedelic sequences. Prompt: `"fisheye lens, extreme wide-angle distortion"`

### Tilt-Shift

Selective focus band that creates a miniature/diorama effect. Use for: establishing shots that feel like models, dreamy cityscapes. Prompt: `"tilt-shift effect, shallow focus band, miniature diorama look"`

### Macro

Extreme close-up capability. Reveals invisible worlds. Dramatic depth separation — even more extreme than telephoto. Use for: texture details, product shots, nature close-ups. Prompt: `"macro lens, extreme close-up detail, razor-thin depth of field"`

## Lens + Emotion

| Want | Lens |
|---|---|
| Epic scale | Ultra wide (14–20mm) |
| Natural documentary | Wide-normal (28–40mm) |
| Intimate portrait | Short telephoto (85mm) |
| Voyeuristic / distant | Long telephoto (200mm+) |
| Cinematic blockbuster | Anamorphic 40–75mm |

## AI Lens Simulation

AI video models approximate lens characteristics based on training data — they don't simulate real optics. Keep in mind:

- **Compression is suggestive, not physical** — A "200mm telephoto" prompt produces the *aesthetic* of compression (flattened planes, tight framing) but not optically accurate results
- **Focal length as style cue** — Think of mm values as shorthand for a look, not a camera setting. The model responds to the visual patterns associated with each lens, not the physics
- **Combine with other cues** — Pairing focal length with depth of field, framing, and subject distance produces more reliable results than focal length alone
